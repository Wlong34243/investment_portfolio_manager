# AMZN — Investment Thesis

## Style
GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price) / Cash Flow Compounder

## Core Thesis
Buying a multi-headed monopoly transitioning from a historic, capital-intensive investment cycle into a massive cash flow harvesting phase. Upside is driven by expanding retail margins (regionalization), the hyper-growth of the high-margin Advertising business, and AWS serving as the foundational infrastructure layer for the AI boom. Amazon is a free cash flow machine. The key question is whether retail margins can sustainably expand while they fund the next generation of AI compute, justifying the premium paid for the stock. Judgment: yes.

## Entry Context
Cost basis: $225.01. At entry, AMZN was trading near highs, reflecting a market that has already validated the initial stages of its retail margin turnaround and AWS's AI-driven re-acceleration. Buying at this level means paying for confirmed execution rather than catching a bottom. The valuation is supported by explosive free cash flow generation, but leaves less room for multiple expansion, making this a pure earnings and cash flow compounding play.

## Bull Case
- The High-Margin Engines: The mix shift in Amazon's business is highly favorable. The fastest-growing segments—AWS (cloud) and Advertising (monetizing high-intent shoppers at the point of sale)—are also the highest margin, structurally lifting the company's overall profitability.
- Retail Margin Expansion: The strategic shift from a national to a regional fulfillment network has drastically reduced the cost-to-serve and improved delivery speeds. The retail segment has moved from a loss-leader to a legitimate, growing profit contributor.
- AWS & AI Infrastructure: AWS remains the undisputed market leader in cloud infrastructure. While AI requires heavy upfront capital, AWS is perfectly positioned to "sell the picks and shovels" (compute, storage, and foundational models via Bedrock) to enterprises building AI applications.
- Free Cash Flow Inflection: As the massive logistics build-out of 2020-2022 moves into the rearview mirror, capital intensity (CapEx as a percentage of revenue) is stabilizing, allowing massive free cash flow to flow to the bottom line.

## Key Risks
- Cloud Competition: Microsoft Azure has aggressively capitalized on its OpenAI partnership. If Azure sustainably outpaces AWS growth, the premium multiple assigned to Amazon's cloud business will compress.
- Macro Sensitivity: The core e-commerce business remains highly exposed to consumer health, inflation, and discretionary spending slowdowns.
- Regulatory / FTC Overhang: Persistent antitrust scrutiny, particularly regarding the bundling of Prime services, fulfillment requirements for third-party sellers, and general monopolistic practices.
- AI CapEx Burden: The capital required to build out frontier AI data centers is staggering. If the revenue payoff takes longer than expected, it could pressure free cash flow in the medium term and punish the stock given the higher entry price.

## Exit Conditions
- AWS growth structurally decelerates well below industry averages, indicating a permanent loss of cloud dominance to Microsoft or Google.
- Retail operating margins structurally regress back toward zero due to rising logistics costs or insurmountable pressure from low-cost entrants (e.g., Temu, Shein).
- FTC successfully forces a severe structural breakup (e.g., decoupling AWS from retail or dismantling the third-party logistics network) that destroys the Prime flywheel ecosystem.
- A clearly superior capital deployment opportunity emerges in the mega-cap space with a better risk/reward profile.
