# NEE — Investment Thesis

## Style
Dividend Growth / Defensive Growth / Regulated Utility + Renewables

## Core Thesis
Buying the premier, "best-of-both-worlds" hybrid utility company. The upside is driven by a unique dual engine: Florida Power & Light (FPL), a highly profitable, regulated monopoly operating in a booming demographic state, and NextEra Energy Resources (NEER), the world's absolute largest generator of renewable energy from the wind and sun. NextEra offers the downside protection of a regulated utility combined with the long-term growth runway of the clean energy transition. The key question is whether they can continue to finance and build their massive renewable backlog in a higher-interest-rate environment. Judgment: yes.

## Entry Context

At $75.37, the valuation reflects a reasonable multiple for a utility that can consistently grow its earnings and dividend at 6–8% annually, offering a much better entry point than its euphoric 2021 highs.

## Bull Case
- **The Florida Demographic Boom:** FPL operates in one of the fastest-growing states in the country. A constant influx of new residents and businesses guarantees structural, organic load growth that most midwestern or northeastern utilities can only dream of.
- **Renewable Juggernaut:** NEER is the undisputed heavyweight champion of green energy development. They have a massive, multi-gigawatt backlog of wind, solar, and battery storage projects, perfectly positioned to capture billions in tax credits from the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- **Constructive Regulation:** FPL enjoys a highly constructive relationship with Florida regulators, allowing them to consistently earn premium returns on equity (ROE) and successfully pass infrastructure upgrade costs (like grid hardening against hurricanes) into their rate base.
- **Dividend Compounder:** NextEra has one of the best dividend-growth track records in the utility sector, offering investors a bond-like yield that actually grows to beat inflation.

## Key Risks
- **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** Utilities are essentially bond proxies and are highly capital intensive. If interest rates stay "higher for longer," NextEra's cost to finance new solar and wind farms increases, compressing margins and making their dividend yield look less attractive compared to risk-free Treasuries.
- **Supply Chain & Tariffs:** Building out a massive renewable portfolio requires imported solar panels and battery components. Tariffs, geopolitical trade wars (especially with China), or supply chain bottlenecks can stall project developments.
- **Regulatory Pushback:** If the political winds in Florida shift and regulators become adversarial (refusing rate hikes or challenging ROEs to protect consumers), FPL’s core earnings engine will stall.
- **Catastrophic Weather:** As a Florida utility, FPL is always one Category 5 hurricane away from billions of dollars in infrastructure damage. While costs are eventually recouped, it creates severe short-term financial and operational stress.

## Exit Conditions
- A permanent, structural shift to a high-inflation, high-interest-rate environment severely breaks the economics of financing long-term renewable energy projects.
- Florida regulators adopt a hostile stance, drastically slashing FPL's allowed Return on Equity (ROE).
- The federal government completely repeals the renewable tax credits (IRA), destroying the margin profile of the NextEra Energy Resources (NEER) segment.
