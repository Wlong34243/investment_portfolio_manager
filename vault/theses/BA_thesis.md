# BA — Investment Thesis

## Style
Deep Value / Turnaround Special Situation

## Core Thesis
Boeing operates within a global, highly entrenched commercial aerospace duopoly alongside Airbus. Despite years of severe self-inflicted wounds—ranging from quality assurance failures to chronic supply chain bottlenecks—the underlying demand for commercial air travel remains insatiable. The core thesis relies on the company's massive, multi-year commercial backlog serving as an unbreakable revenue floor. As new management stabilizes the production line, resolves FAA oversight issues, and returns aircraft deliveries to historical run rates, Boeing is primed for a massive free cash flow inflection.

## Entry Context
Cost basis: $215.00.
Halftime Committee Perspective: At this entry, the CNBC Halftime Report Investment Committee’s value faction views Boeing as a classic "show-me" turnaround with an outsized price-to-value disconnect. The committee consensus argues that while the immediate P/E ratio is virtually unreadable due to depressed near-term earnings, the forward Price-to-Free-Cash-Flow becomes incredibly cheap once 737 and 787 deliveries normalize. They view $215 as an attractive margin-of-safety entry point, buying a multi-year backlog of guaranteed revenue at a historically depressed valuation multiple while waiting for execution to catch up.

## Bull Case
The Duopoly Moat: Airlines have practically no alternative to Boeing and Airbus. Switching costs are astronomical due to pilot training and maintenance infrastructure, meaning airlines cannot easily abandon Boeing without crippling their own growth plans.

Backlog Realization: Boeing sits on a backlog of thousands of commercial aircraft. Converting this backlog into deliveries directly translates to a surge in deferred free cash flow.

Defense & Space Floor: While commercial aviation is the primary driver, the Defense, Space & Security segment provides a steady stream of U.S. government-backed revenue that helps stabilize cash flow during commercial down-cycles.

## Key Risks
Execution & Safety: Another catastrophic safety incident or grounding of a major aircraft family would severely damage the brand and halt the financial recovery.

Labor Relations: Prolonged labor union strikes or severe wage inflation could stall production lines and eat into the already thin recovery margins.

Airbus Encroachment: Airbus could capitalize on Boeing's stumbles to permanently capture an insurmountable share of the highly lucrative narrow-body (single-aisle) market.

## Exit Conditions
- Management repeatedly fails to hit stated production and delivery run-rates over a 12-to-18-month period.
- A severe liquidity crisis or catastrophic liability event forces highly dilutive equity raises.
- Airbus permanently secures over 65% of the global narrow-body market, structurally degrading Boeing's long-term pricing power.
