# KTOS — Investment Thesis

## Core Thesis (Thematic “Expendable Airpower” View)

I added Kratos Defense at 6% as a new position and a deliberate sector diversification. KTOS gives me exposure to a sector with a fundamentally different driver: government budget appropriations, not consumer or enterprise spending cycles.

The thesis has two layers:

1.  **The Attributable Drone Franchise**: Kratos makes the Valkyrie, Firejet, and Spartan systems that represent the Pentagon's bet on expendable, affordable airpower. While KTOS lost CCA Increment 1 earlier this year, I believe that loss is now fully priced. The market treated the CCA loss as a verdict on KTOS's entire drone program; it is not. The broader drone procurement cycle is expanding, evidenced by the FY2027 DoD budget proposing $75B in drone spending and a $200B Iran supplemental markup.
2.  **Hypersonic Test Infrastructure**: The MACH-TB contract ($1.45B ceiling) and the $446M Space Systems Command contract provide a cash-generating legacy business. This infrastructure funds the venture-style bets in autonomous systems, ensuring KTOS isn't a pure "speculative play" on drones winning a single competition.

## Valuation & Risk Targets

KTOS is not a "cheap" stock by traditional metrics; it is an asymmetric bet on a structural shift in warfare.

- **Forward P/E**: ~85x (elevated, reflects growth premium).
- **Cash Flow**: Negative $150M to $200M FCF expected in FY2026.
- **Dependency**: Requires the Pentagon budget to materialize as proposed and for the "affordable mass" doctrine to transition from concept to multi-year procurement.
- **Catalysts**: May 6 Q1 earnings is the first major in-window catalyst for validation.

## Position Sizing & Action Zones

Sized at **6.0%**, the midpoint of my sizing range. This reflects medium conviction with genuine upside asymmetry.
