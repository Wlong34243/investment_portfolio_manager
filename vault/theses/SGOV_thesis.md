# SGOV — Investment Thesis

## Style
Cash Management / Capital Preservation / Risk-Free Yield

## Core Thesis
Buying supreme liquidity and absolute capital preservation while getting paid the risk-free rate to wait. SGOV (iShares 0-3 Month Treasury Bond ETF) is not an investment for capital appreciation; it is a strategic cash proxy. The upside is entirely tied to earning the prevailing short-term Treasury yield without taking on duration risk or corporate credit risk. It serves as "dry powder," providing the psychological and financial flexibility to aggressively buy equities when market drawdowns occur. The key question is simply whether the current yield justifies the opportunity cost of not being fully invested in risk assets. Judgment: yes, as a tactical portfolio buffer.

## Entry Context
Cost basis: ~$100. SGOV trades in a tight band around par, oscillating slightly as interest accrues and is paid out monthly. Buying at this level is entirely agnostic to market timing—it is simply a vehicle to park capital. The entry reflects a conscious decision to secure a high-quality, short-term yield rather than holding zero-interest cash or reaching for yield in riskier corporate paper.

## Bull Case
- **Absolute Safety:** The underlying assets are ultra-short-term US Treasury bills (0-3 months), meaning credit risk is virtually zero. It is as close to a risk-free asset as exists in the financial system.
- **Zero Duration Risk:** Unlike longer-term bond funds (e.g., TLT or AGG) that suffer severe capital losses when interest rates rise, SGOV's ultra-short duration means its principal value is effectively immune to interest rate shocks.
- **Supreme Liquidity & Optionality:** It trades with massive daily volume, allowing instant liquidation to deploy capital into equities or other asset classes the moment a high-conviction opportunity arises.
- **Tax Efficiency:** The monthly dividend distributions generated by SGOV are generally exempt from state and local income taxes, giving it a higher tax-equivalent yield compared to standard high-yield savings accounts or CDs (depending on your tax jurisdiction).

## Key Risks
- **Opportunity Cost (Cash Drag):** Holding a large allocation in SGOV during a sustained, raging equity bull market will structurally drag down overall portfolio returns over the long term.
- **Reinvestment / Rate Cut Risk:** SGOV's yield mechanically tracks the Fed Funds rate. If the Federal Reserve aggressively cuts interest rates, SGOV's yield will drop immediately, removing the income incentive of the position.
- **Inflation Risk:** While the nominal yield is secure, if inflation spikes structurally higher than the short-term Treasury rate, the real, inflation-adjusted return of this position becomes negative, eroding purchasing power.
