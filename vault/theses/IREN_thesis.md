# IREN — Investment Thesis

## Style
Hyper-Growth / Speculative Tech / Green AI Compute

## Core Thesis
Buying a highly volatile, next-generation infrastructure company leveraging 100% renewable energy to power the two most energy-intensive industries on earth: Bitcoin mining and AI cloud compute. Upside is driven by the aggressive buildout of data centers specifically designed for Nvidia H100/B200 GPUs, funded by cash flows from their Bitcoin mining fleet. Iris Energy is a bet that hyperscalers will pay a massive premium for green, immediately available megawatt capacity. The key question is whether they can secure enough high-margin AI cloud contracts to justify the staggering capital expenditures required for the hardware. Judgment: yes, as a high-risk growth allocation.

## Entry Context
Cost basis: $33.22. Buying at this level reflects extreme optimism regarding the company's AI pivot. At nearly $40, IREN is being priced as a legitimate AI cloud provider, not just a crypto miner. This entry requires management to flawlessly execute their GPU deployment strategy and secure massive, long-term cloud service agreements to prevent the valuation from collapsing.

## Bull Case
- **The "Green" Premium:** Big Tech companies have massive ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) mandates. They are desperate for compute, but they cannot afford to ruin their carbon-neutral pledges by using coal-powered data centers. IREN’s 100% renewable power sites offer a highly attractive, premium solution.
- **Speed to Market:** Because IREN owns its land, substations, and power infrastructure outright, they can deploy new AI clusters months or years faster than competitors waiting on utility grid approvals.
- **Nvidia Partnership:** IREN has successfully secured and deployed massive clusters of Nvidia GPUs. By operating as a boutique AI cloud, they can charge premium hourly rates for AI training and inferencing to startups and enterprises who are locked out of AWS or Azure.
- **Bitcoin Optionality:** The core Bitcoin mining business still operates at a highly efficient cost basis due to their cheap, renewable power, providing a baseline of cash generation if the AI pivot takes longer than expected.

## Key Risks
- **Capital Intensity & Dilution:** Buying thousands of Nvidia GPUs costs billions. IREN constantly risks diluting shareholders through at-the-market (ATM) stock offerings to fund these purchases. If the AI revenue doesn't scale fast enough, the dilution will click the stock price.
- **Hardware Obsolescence:** The AI hardware cycle moves incredibly fast. If IREN buys massive amounts of current-generation GPUs, and a new, vastly superior chip is released 12 months later, their hardware depreciates rapidly and lease rates plummet.
- **Hyperscaler Competition:** As Amazon, Google, and Microsoft eventually catch up on their own data center buildouts, the acute shortage of GPU compute will end, likely crashing the sky-high lease rates IREN currently relies on.

## Exit Conditions
- Management relies exclusively on massive shareholder dilution (stock issuance) to fund operations because they cannot secure debt financing.
- AI cloud lease rates collapse due to an oversupply of GPUs in the broader market, destroying the projected return on investment for their data centers.
- The company fails to secure binding, long-term cloud service agreements with reputable AI companies, relying entirely on volatile month-to-month spot pricing.
