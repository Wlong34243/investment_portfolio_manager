# RSP — Investment Thesis

## Style
Passive Core / Smart Beta / Anti-Concentration

## Core Thesis
Buying the underlying earnings power of the top 500 American companies without taking on the extreme, top-heavy concentration risk of the traditional market-cap-weighted S&P 500. Upside is driven by the broader market participating in economic growth—particularly value, industrials, and mid-cap stocks—rather than relying solely on the "Magnificent Seven" tech monopolies. The key question is whether the average American mega-cap can consistently grow earnings without being entirely eclipsed by the sheer scale of Big Tech. Judgment: yes, as a structural diversifier.

## Entry Context
Cost basis: ~$189.32 (Assumed). Buying RSP is a strategic asset allocation decision to de-risk a portfolio. If your portfolio already holds massive individual allocations to mega-cap tech (like your MSFT, META, and QQQM positions), buying RSP acts as a perfect counterweight. You are intentionally trading some top-heavy tech momentum for structural, broad-market safety. 

## Bull Case
- **Anti-Fragility:** In the traditional S&P 500 (SPY), a 10% drop in Apple or Microsoft drags the entire index down with it. In RSP, Apple is given the exact same 0.20% weighting as Ralph Lauren or Campbell Soup. This completely insulates the fund from a targeted mega-cap tech crash.
- **The "Buy Low, Sell High" Mechanic:** Because RSP is equal-weighted, it is forced to rebalance quarterly. This means it mechanically trims the stocks that have gone up the most (taking profits) and buys more of the stocks that have dropped (buying the dip), enforcing a disciplined value-tilt.
- **Broad Market Breadth:** When the economy enters a true, healthy expansion, market rallies "broaden out" beyond just AI and tech. RSP captures the explosive upside of industrials, financials, and healthcare companies that standard indexes ignore.
- **Valuation Discount:** Historically, the equal-weight index trades at a more reasonable Price-to-Earnings (P/E) multiple than the market-cap index, offering a better margin of safety.

## Key Risks
- **Chronic Underperformance in Tech Bull Markets:** If a handful of tech monopolies continue to eat the entire global economy, RSP will mechanically and severely underperform the standard S&P 500 because it consistently sells its tech winners to buy slower-growing companies.
- **Slightly Higher Fees:** At an expense ratio of 0.20%, RSP is slightly more expensive to hold than a nearly free market-cap fund like VOO (0.03%), which can cause a minor drag over a multi-decade horizon.
- **Mid-Cap Sensitivity:** Because RSP shifts weight away from the massive mega-caps, it acts a bit more like a mid-cap fund. This makes it slightly more sensitive to higher interest rates and domestic economic slowdowns.
