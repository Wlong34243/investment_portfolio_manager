# NOW — Investment Thesis

## Style
Hyper-Growth SaaS / Enterprise Workflow / Legacy Compounder

## Core Thesis
Holding the "operating system" for the modern enterprise. Upside is driven by near-zero churn, massive land-and-expand revenue models, and the aggressive monetization of AI tools (Now Assist) embedded directly into white-collar workflows. ServiceNow started as an IT ticketing system and has successfully expanded into HR, customer service, and creator workflows. The key question is whether corporate IT budgets can absorb the premium pricing of their new AI-enabled tiers without forcing vendor consolidation. Judgment: yes.

## Entry Context
Cost basis: $103.13. Like Netflix, this is a phenomenal legacy entry. You bought a best-in-class software compounder early in its growth curve. At this basis, you are virtually immune to the daily multiple-contraction volatility that plagues the SaaS sector. The thesis is to hold the asset as it scales into a generational tech behemoth alongside Microsoft and Salesforce.

## Bull Case
- **Incredible Stickiness:** Once an enterprise integrates ServiceNow to manage its internal IT and employee onboarding, it is practically impossible to rip out. This results in renewal rates consistently sitting around 98-99%.
- **The AI Monetization Catalyst:** ServiceNow is perfectly positioned to actually charge for AI. By embedding generative AI (Now Assist) into workflows, they save corporations thousands of man-hours, easily justifying the massive price hikes for their "Pro" and "Plus" tiers.
- **Land and Expand:** They are masters of upselling. A customer might start by buying the IT module, and three years later, they are paying for HR, legal, and customer service modules, structurally driving up the lifetime value of the account.
- **Margin Expansion:** As the massive, upfront sales and marketing costs required to acquire these Fortune 500 customers scale down relative to revenue, massive free cash flow is unleashed to the bottom line.

## Key Risks
- **Valuation / Multiple Compression:** While your cost basis is completely safe, the stock itself trades at a massive premium to the market. Any slight deceleration in top-line growth (e.g., dropping from 22% to 18%) can cause violent sell-offs in the share price.
- **Vendor Consolidation:** In a tough macroeconomic environment, Chief Information Officers (CIOs) look to cut software spend. If Microsoft aggressively bundles competing workflow tools into Office 365 for free, it could threaten ServiceNow's pricing power.
- **Execution at Scale:** As the company crosses $10B+ in revenue, maintaining the hyper-growth culture and avoiding bureaucratic slowdowns becomes the primary internal challenge for management.

next_step: hold

## Exit Conditions
- Gross renewal rates structurally drop below 95%, indicating the product is losing its "sticky" moat and customers are ripping it out.
- Microsoft or Salesforce successfully launch and scale a directly competitive IT/workflow product that forces ServiceNow into a margin-crushing price war.
- The CEO and core leadership team depart, causing a massive shift in corporate strategy and execution.
