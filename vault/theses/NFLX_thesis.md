# NFLX — Investment Thesis

## Style
Mega-Cap Tech / Streaming Monopoly / Legacy Compounder

## Core Thesis
Holding the undisputed victor of the global streaming wars. Upside is driven by massive, structural free cash flow generation, immense pricing power, and the high-margin scaling of their advertising tier. Netflix out-spent, out-scaled, and out-lasted legacy media. It has transitioned from a cash-burning growth story into a highly profitable media utility. The key question is whether their pivot into live sports and gaming can sustain engagement as subscription prices continue to rise. Judgment: yes, hold the winner.

## Entry Context
Cost basis: $91.22. This is a legacy, deep-in-the-money position. Buying in the $90s means you captured the stock years ago. You have survived the massive drawdowns and password-sharing panics, and are now holding a cash-printing machine. The thesis here is strictly about *not interrupting the compounding*. You do not sell a monopoly unless the moat breaks.

## Bull Case
- **The Wars Are Over:** Legacy media competitors (Disney, Paramount, Warner) have waved the white flag, slashing their content budgets and returning to licensing their best shows *to* Netflix. Netflix is the default television interface for the globe.
- **Advertising Cash Machine:** The rollout of the ad-supported tier provides a massive secondary revenue engine. It allows them to monetize price-sensitive consumers while offering high-margin, highly targeted inventory to global advertisers.
- **Pricing Power:** Netflix has repeatedly proven it can raise subscription prices and crack down on password sharing with minimal churn. Their content library is simply too sticky to cancel.
- **Live Events & Sports:** The pivot into live programming (like the $5B WWE Raw deal) locks in a new demographic of viewers, ensures appointment viewing, and makes their advertising inventory infinitely more valuable.

## Key Risks
- **Content Saturation / Fatigue:** If Netflix fails to consistently produce cultural hits (like *Stranger Things* or *Squid Game*), engagement will eventually wane, leading to subscriber stagnation.
- **Capital Allocation:** As they generate billions in free cash flow, there is a risk management misallocates it—such as aggressively overpaying for live sports rights or burning billions on a failed push into AAA video gaming.
- **Macro Sensitivity:** While viewed as a utility, multiple price hikes have made the premium tiers expensive. A severe global recession could force consumers to trade down to the ad tier or pause subscriptions.

## Scaling State
next_step: hold

## Rotation Priority
priority: very low (never sell a compounding monopoly without a broken thesis)

## Exit Conditions
- Core subscriber engagement (hours viewed) drops structurally for multiple consecutive quarters.
- Management completely abandons financial discipline, returning to massive negative free cash flow to fund low-return initiatives.
- A disruptive, paradigm-shifting technology (like interactive AI-generated media) fundamentally breaks the passive video streaming business model.
