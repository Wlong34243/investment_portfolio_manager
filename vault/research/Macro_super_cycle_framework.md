# macro_super_cycle_framework

* **Phase Paradigm:** Carlota Perez Techno-Economic Super Cycle Framework
* **Primary Objective:** Identification of actionable economic, financial, and behavioral indicators tracking the life cycle of technological revolutions.

### 1. Installation Phase (Financial Capital Dominance)
The first half of the super-cycle, characterized by the aggressive deployment of a new technology paradigm, deep structural economic divergence, and the dominance of financial capital.

**A. Irruption Phase**
*   **Sectoral Divergence:** Explosive productivity, market penetration, and extraordinary profit margins in emerging technology sectors contrasting sharply with stagnant productivity, shrinking markets, and rising unemployment in mature industries.
*   **Price Instability:** Deflation in traditional constrained markets or runaway inflation, depending on the macroeconomic framework, alongside chaotic relative price structures across the economy.
*   **Capital Allocation:** Surge in the formation of venture capital and risk capital instruments explicitly funding new technological entrepreneurs.
*   **Corporate Rejuvenation:** Mature, cash-rich corporations initiate early, high-risk pilot investments into the new technological paradigm to reverse declining profit trends.

**B. Frenzy Phase**
*   **Financial Decoupling:** The financial (paper) economy completely decouples from the real production economy, with financial entities seeking short-term capital gains rather than dividend yields.
*   **Asset Inflation:** Rampant asset inflation, stock market bubbles, and soaring price/earnings ratios fundamentally disconnected from actual corporate earnings.
*   **Wealth Polarization:** Extreme concentration of wealth at the top of the income spectrum and widening social inequality.
*   **Infrastructural Overcapacity:** Intense, speculative over-investment in the new foundational infrastructure, leading to rapid build-outs that exceed current utilization capacity.
*   **Oligopoly Formation:** Fierce price competition triggered by rapid technological obsolescence leads to early waves of defensive mergers, acquisitions, and the formation of industry oligopolies.
*   **Institutional Opacity:** Flourishing of unorthodox financial practices, leveraging, and regulatory evasion, exploiting the lag in the institutional framework.

### 2. The Turning Point / Crash (Transition Crossroads)
The transitional crisis connecting the Installation and Deployment phases, characterized by the unsustainability of the financial bubble and a forced structural macroeconomic realignment.

**Financial Signals**
*   **Asset Price Implosion:** Sudden bursting of stock market bubbles and extreme contraction of inflated paper asset valuations.
*   **Systemic Bankruptcies:** Cascading failures and bankruptcies among highly leveraged financial entities, venture funds, and speculative infrastructure projects.
*   **Value Convergence:** Violent realignment of paper wealth valuations to accurately reflect the real wealth-generating capacity of the underlying production economy.

**Fundamental Market Signals**
*   **Macroeconomic Contraction:** Onset of a severe, system-wide economic recession or depression.
*   **Premature Market Saturation:** Potential production capacity outstrips effective market demand due to extreme income concentration limiting the consumer base.
*   **Index Reversion:** The widening gap between nominal stock market indices and real GDP growth reaches a breaking point and violently reverses.

**Behavioral Signals**
*   **Shift in Investment Criteria:** Capital allocators abandon short-term capital gains metrics in favor of long-term, dividend-producing capacity and real profit generation.
*   **Regulatory Demands:** Immediate loss of confidence in financial deregulation, triggering intense political and social pressure for state intervention, transparency, and strict financial market regulation.
*   **Institutional Recomposition:** Creation of new institutional frameworks and labor/social policies designed to redistribute income and balance private profit with collective social stability.

### 3. Deployment Phase (Production Capital Dominance)
The second half of the super-cycle, characterized by the widespread integration of the new technological paradigm across the entire economy, driven by the long-term criteria of production capital.

**C. Synergy Phase ("Golden Age")**
*   **Consistent Real Growth:** Resumption of steady, harmonious macroeconomic expansion with rising, widespread employment.
*   **Market Expansion:** Rapid expansion of mass consumer markets, driven by institutional income redistribution, social safety nets, and broader middle-class purchasing power.
*   **Financial Recoupling:** Financial capital acts strictly as a facilitator/intermediary, supporting the real expansion of production capital rather than engaging in autonomous speculation.
*   **Price Stabilization:** Relative productivities across the economy equalize, leading to the stabilization of relative price structures and the return of a single, coherent money value.
*   **Process Innovation Focus:** R&D and innovation shift from exploratory product breakthroughs to process improvements and cost reductions, aiming to achieve massive economies of scale.

**D. Maturity Phase**
*   **Productivity Stagnation:** Diminishing returns on technological innovation, flattening productivity curves, and declining profit margins in the paradigm's core industries.
*   **Market Saturation:** Hard saturation of consumer and industrial markets for the dominant paradigm's products, leading to sluggish sales growth.
*   **Capital Outflows (Redeployment):** Accumulation of idle corporate capital seeking yield, leading to massive foreign direct investment and out-migration of manufacturing to peripheral/developing countries.
*   **Debt Cycle Gestation:** Excess idle capital floods into high-risk sovereign debt, consumer credit, and questionable financial instruments, planting the seeds for future debt crises and defaults.
*   **Market Concentration:** A renewed wave of corporate mergers and acquisitions driven not by innovation, but by the necessity to capture market share in a shrinking, zero-sum environment.
*   **Social Unrest:** Resurgence of labor strikes, political unrest, and social protests stemming from stagnating real wages and unfulfilled economic expectations.
*   **Radical R&D Funding:** Established corporations and idle financial capital begin lifting the implicit ban on unorthodox technologies, funding radical innovations to break out of the stagnation trap, thus incubating the next technological revolution.
